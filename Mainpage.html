Welcome to Bad Habits


Do you have a bad habit?
Currently, your answer might be no. However, we want you to evaluate your daily engagement in certain activities (ex. eating cookies, drinking coffee, smoking cigarettes, etc.), now compare that number to your ideal engagement with these activities. Now tell us, do you have a bad habit?

Our app targets thoughtful decision makers who are adamant about positively altering their lives. By maintaining daily visits to our web app, “Bad Habits” will keep track of your engagement goal (user’s set their goals in our intro survey) then will compare that number to your actual level of engagement; which will allow you to decide whether or not you have a bad habit.

Our app sends out helpful reminders, alternative’s to that bad habit, and keeps track of your progress.

Are you ready to break your bad habit?



This app is not guaranteed to end your addiction. For more serious addictions, please seek out professional help.

Help/FAQs

Why is this app important?

“Bad Habits” aims to convert weaknesses into strengths. This app acts as a helping aid for our users to acknowledge, target, and solve their bad habits.

How does it work?

After signing up, users will be directed to a survey gathering information about daily personal habits. The survey provides the building blocks needed to successfully conquer bad habits. Based on the survey responses, our app will track your personal long-term/short-term goals, progress, alert preferences, provide resources and alternatives to this substance.

What if my bad habit is not listed?

We do have another option where users can manually input their bad habit. We do not have a page specifically for your addiction yet, however, you will be able to track your progress and set alert reminders.

Can I have more than one bad habit?

You can keep track of two bad habits, however, we suggest you focus on strengthening one bad habit at a time.

Will this app help end my addiction?

Yes, however, our users are completely responsible for the amount of effort put forth. In order to successfully accomplish set goals; our users must adopt these two guidelines:

  • I will hold myself accountable for my progress...
  • I have enough willpower to successfully accomplish my goal(s)...

Further Developments

We recognize that this program is not quite complex. Ideally we'll be able to expand this program to include many more features, such as being catered to more addictions/bad habits, having options for different languages, and having constant alerts to the users to remind them not to relapse. Our ultimate goal though is to link our program with the users' bank account and create a goals and rewards tab. This way, the user can set goals for themselves each week, and when the goals are accomplished, the user is treated to a reward. For every week the goal is reached, our program will set aside a set amount of money, determined by the user, into an account which the user can then access to treat themselves to the reward. If the goal is related to budgeting and at the end of the week the user is under budget, the remaining money in the budget will be placed in the rewards account. Also, we plan to make this web application and application for mobile devices.
